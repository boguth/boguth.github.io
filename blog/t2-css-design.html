CSS Blog - March 26, 2015

Alrighty! Here we tackle the concept of borders, padding, and margins.

Let's start with border. That's a simple one. A border simply marks a section of content. Usually, a border is square. One property of borders is that they can be thick or thin, dotten or solid, or simply invisible.

Margin specifies the amount of space in between the border and another element. It's essentially a way of selecting the space between.

Within the borders of an element is the content. Padding specifies the amount of space in between the content and the border.
