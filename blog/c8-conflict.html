May 9th - Conflict

  1. Discuss a time you had a conflict. What was it about? What happened?
  2. What was the source of the conflict?
  3. What basic emotions did you feel upon analyzing the conflict?
  4. What actions did you take to resolve your conflict?
  5. If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?
  6. What did you learn from this experience?

1. I had a conflict with a pairing partner over whether to use divs or unordered lists. He presented the former, I countered with the latter.
2. The source of the conflict was over which ideas we presented, and more essentially, pride over which idea was correct.
3. Upon analyzing the conflict I realized I felt frustrated, because I knew that in this particular situation, that my idea made more sense. I also felt anxious, because I worried that my partner would be defensive if I insisted on my idea.
4. In order to resolve the conflict, I presented my reasoning. I kept the issue centered on me: "Well, to me it makes sense because..." rather than focusing on his idea.
5. I don't think I would go back to try anything different on this conflict. By suggesting my reasoning, my partner saw my point of view, and we eventually ended up doing it my way. I felt good about it, not because I was "right," but because the conflict was resolved amicably.
6. I learned that, often, presenting your side of an idea without adding judgment value - i.e. "I'm right, you're wrong" - can be a valuable technique in a conflict.
